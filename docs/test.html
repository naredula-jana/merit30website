Merit-30

Free EAMCET/IIT Coaching Center for government school(ZPHS) meritorious students

Mission Statement:

“Education for all is the highest form of service.” Guided by this belief, we are committed to transforming the lives of families below the poverty line (BPL), empowering them to move from relying on government subsidies to becoming self-reliant, tax-paying citizens. Our mission is to provide free, high-quality coaching to talented students from Zilla Parishad High Schools (ZPHS) to help them excel in competitive exams like EAPCET and IIT-JEE, enabling admission to premier colleges at no cost. By fostering academic excellence and opening doors to rewarding career opportunities, we strive to uplift individuals, families, and communities, paving the way for a brighter future.

Empowering ZPHS Students: Transforming Lives through Free Engineering Coaching:

By selecting meritorious students from multiple Zilla Parishad High Schools (ZPHS) and providing free coaching for EAPCET/IIT, we aim to achieve the following goals:
  1. Achieving strong EAPCET/IIT ranks: By helping students secure high ranks, we will uplift the BPL families of these children, transforming their lives from being dependent on government subsidies to becoming dignified, tax-paying citizens.
  2. Inspiring other students from ZPHS to pursue EAPCET/IIT: Currently, the number of students from ZPHS who achieve EAPCET/IIT ranks is extremely low. By demonstrating success through our selected students, we aim to motivate a larger group of students to participate in the exams, creating a ripple effect that encourages many others in ZPHS schools to aspire for higher education and better opportunities.

Activities:

Currently, Merit-30 runs two initiatives under its free EAPCET/IIT coaching program and plans to introduce a third in the April-2025 :
  1. Free EAPCET/IIT Coaching for 9th and 10th Grades: Merit-30 provides free coaching for 9th and 10th-grade students after school hours (5:30 PM to 7:30 PM). Around 60 students from five Zilla Parishad High Schools (ZPHS) are currently benefiting from these sessions.:
    • Nizampet ZPHS school( distance=1.8km): Nizampet ZPHS to Center.
    • Hydernagar ZPHS school( distance=3km): Hydernagar ZPHS to Center.
    • Bachpally ZPHS school ( distance=5.6km) Bachupally ZPHS school to center
    • Shamshiguda ZPHS school (distance=4.7km) Shamsiguda ZPHS school
    • Miyapur ZPHS school:(distance=4.3km) Miyapur ZPHS school
  2. Free Boys Hostel for IIT Batch: We currently offer hostel facilities to a select group of boys from the 9th and 10th-grade IIT batch. In the future, this support may be expanded to include students in 11th and 12th grades.
  3. Full-Time Free Junior College for 11th and 12th Grades with Integrated EAPCET/IIT Coaching: Beginning in the academic year 2025-26(April-2025), we will establish a full-time free junior college for 11th and 12th grades, featuring integrated coaching for EAPCET/IIT exams. Currently, we are coaching two batches of 10th-grade students for EAPCET/IIT, who will advance to the junior college program in 11th grade next year.

Background

Founder: Merit-30 is an NGO founded by an IITian Janardhana Reddy Naredula in the year 2002. It has been committed to provide various educational services to children from BPL families. Here is a brief journey overview of NGO:

Phases of Growth of Merit-30(NGO):

  1. Phase-1 (2002-2008, Kurnool): Orphan center: Supported orphan children with shelter, food, and education.
  2. Phase-2 (2008-2022, Bangalore): Financial Support: Extended financial support to government school students and provided tuition fees.
  3. Phase-3 (2022-Present, Hyderabad): Merit-30: Free EAMCET/IIT coaching: Launched free EAMCET/IIT coaching center for ZPHS schools.

Evolution of Our Services over the period of time :

Over the years, Merit-30 model of service is evolved and learned from various projects, shifting approach to maximize Merit-30 impact. Initially, NGO is to provide education, food, and shelter alone. which limited our reach to a small number of children. Recently, the service model is transformed to function as a catalyst, focusing on providing free coaching to a larger number of government school students. This new approach strengthens government schools and impacts to a broader section of children from BPL.
